When the aragonite saturation state sis below about 1.5 it is likely that larval oysters will not survive because they will be unable to form shells. During upwelling conditions (winds from the north, typically in the summer) the deep waters entering the coastal estuaries from the shelf can be relatively "corrosive" (low aragonite saturation state) because of natural and anthropogenic processes.
